# Friedrich Moch (GER)
**Bib:** 8-2 (Leg 2, Classic) | **FIS Code:** 3200802 | **DOB:** 12 April 2000 | **Age:** 25
**Club/Team:** WSV Isny / Zoll Ski Team | **National Team Tier:** A Team (2025/2026)

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Born in Memmingen, Germany. Grew up in Isny in the Württemberg Allgäu region. Currently lives in Oberstdorf. [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)
- **Family Background:** Son of a pediatrician in Isny. Has three siblings, including younger brother Jakob Moch (born 2006), who is also a very successful cross-country skier (2x Youth Olympic gold medalist, 2025 Junior Worlds bronze). His older sister and younger brother are not athletes and are studying. Father Alexander Moch once said Friedrich's talent and will to win came from his great-grandfather, who once won a medal at Oberjoch. [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Unlike many Scandinavian skiers, Moch did not start cross-country skiing at kindergarten age. As a child, "Frie" was at home on alpine skis and the football pitch. He didn't try cross-country skiing until age twelve, making his early career successes all the more impressive. [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)
- **Partner / Relationship:** Partner is fellow cross-country skier Lena Keck. They have been together since their school days and have lived together in Oberstdorf since autumn 2023. [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)

## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Two-time Olympian (Beijing 2022, Milano Cortina 2026). Best finish: 5th in Men's 4x10km Relay at Beijing 2022; 13th in skiathlon at Beijing. [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/friedrich-moch)
- **World Championship results:** Three World Championships participations. Bronze in Men's 4x10km Relay at Planica 2023 (secured the result for Germany against France on the anchor leg). [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 4 podiums — 1 x 2nd, 3 x 3rd. [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/friedrich-moch)
- **Tour de Ski:** 2nd overall in Tour de Ski (previous season, behind Harald Østberg Amundsen), which was the foundation for his 6th-place finish in both the overall World Cup and distance World Cup standings. [source](https://www.xc-ski.de/events/langlauf-weltcup/portraits/national/moch-friedrich-portrait/)
- **Junior World Championships:** 2 x silver, 1 x bronze. [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/friedrich-moch)
- **Awards:** 2025 Golden Ski award winner in German cross-country skiing. [source](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/cross-country-skier-in-doping-case-named-athlete-of-the-year-2025/)

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Classic strength:** Very strong — his Tour de Ski 2nd-place finish was built on strong classic legs. Assigned to Leg 2 (Classic) in the relay.
- **Freestyle strength:** Capable; started career more oriented toward alpine skiing and skate skiing.
- **Sprint ability:** Limited — sprint results have been poor this season (75th at TdS sprint).
- **Distance preference:** Distance specialist, particularly 10km-50km.
- **Best format:** Interval start / pursuit / multi-stage tours.
- **Climbing ability:** Strong — Tour de Ski Final Climb results demonstrate climbing ability.

## Key Storylines
- Dramatic fall in form from last season (Tour de Ski 2nd, WC overall 6th) to this season (Tour de Ski 47th). Only 5 top-15 finishes before the Tour de Ski this season.
- Brother Jakob Moch (born 2006) is also on the German team and competed in the Tour de Ski at age 19 — a family rivalry/partnership dynamic.
- Named 2025 German Athlete of the Year in cross-country skiing despite the doping controversy surrounding the award.
- Coach Schlickenrieder's bold prediction of future world/Olympic champion adds pressure.
- His assignment to Leg 2 (Classic) is strategic, leveraging his proven classic ability despite poor overall season form.
