## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** First Olympic appearance at Milano Cortina 2026. Best finish: 18th in Men's Skiathlon. [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/results.html?sectorcode=CC&raceid=49723)
- **World Championship results:** Two participations (Planica 2023, Trondheim 2025). **Silver medal** in Men's 4x10km Relay at Planica 2023 (at age 20 — youngest member of the Finnish relay team). 24th in 15km at Planica. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niko_Anttola)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** No podiums. Best result: 13th in Davos. Broke into top 20 for first time in March 2024 in Lahti (19th in 20km). [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niko_Anttola)
- **Junior World Championships:** **1 x gold** (10km, 2023), **3 x silver** (4x5km relay 2021, 10km 2022, 20km 2023). [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niko_Anttola)
- **European Youth Olympic Festival:** Gold in 7.5km classic and 10km freestyle. Bronze in 4x5km mixed relay. [source](https://www.renta.fi/en/sustainability/sponsorship/team-renta/niko-anttola/)
- **World Cup debut:** February 2022 in Lahti, collecting first WC points during 2022-23 season. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niko_Anttola)

## Key Storylines
- At just 23, the youngest member of the Finnish relay team — Olympic debut.
- Already has a **World Championship relay silver** (Planica 2023) at age 20, proving he can perform in relay format.
- Coached by his father Marko — maintaining a family dynamic at the Olympic level.
- Spectacular junior career (1 gold, 3 silver at Junior Worlds; EYOF double gold) makes him one of Finland's most anticipated young talents.
- Assigned to the pressure-filled anchor leg (Leg 4, Freestyle) despite being the youngest — a sign of the Finnish team's confidence in him.
- His 18th-place finish in the Olympic skiathlon was respectable for a first-timer but not outstanding.
- Represents the next generation of Finnish distance skiing after Niskanen.
