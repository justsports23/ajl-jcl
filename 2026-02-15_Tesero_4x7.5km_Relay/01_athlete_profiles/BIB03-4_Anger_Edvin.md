# Edvin Anger (SWE)
**Bib:** 3-4 | **FIS Code:** 3501380 | **DOB:** 2001-12-03 | **Age:** 24
**Club/Team:** Duveds IF | **National Team Tier:** A-team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Born in Rensbo, near Grådö, in Hedemora kommun, Dalarna. Grew up in the Hedemora area and has deep roots there. After graduating from the ski academy, he moved back home to Hedemora rather than relocating to a larger training center. He briefly moved to Östersund with his then-girlfriend in 2023 but has maintained strong ties to Hedemora. He initially competed for IFK Hedemora SK before switching to Åsarna IK around 2021 to advance his career. [source](https://nyheter24.se/sport/1130654-allt-om-edvin-anger-langd-familj-och-vm-i-langdskidor-2023) [source](https://www.sodran.se/artikel/familjetragedin-som-formade-edvin-forlorade-sin-lillasyster-blev-omtanksam/)
- **Family Background:** Parents are Fredrik and Ellinor Anger, both from Hedemora. Father Fredrik competed in cross-country skiing at a high level (just below the national team) and ran the Vasaloppet. Fredrik now runs a sports shop in Hedemora, while mother Ellinor runs a flower shop. He has a younger brother, Pelle, who also competes in cross-country skiing. The family suffered a tragedy when Edvin was three years old: his baby sister Stina died at just six months of age, and his paternal grandfather also passed away around the same time. His mother has said the loss of Stina shaped Edvin into "an empathetic person." The family opened up about these traumatic events in a Viaplay documentary. His aunt, Sofia Anger, chairs his official fan club. The family has a farm connection -- Edvin has said he dreamed of being a farmer as a child and driving a tractor. [source](https://www.sodran.se/artikel/familjetragedin-som-formade-edvin-forlorade-sin-lillasyster-blev-omtanksam/) [source](https://lokalti.se/edvin-siktar-pa-os-medalj-foraldrarna-foljer-masterskapet-pa-plats/) [source](https://www.sodran.se/2024-01-04/angers-familj-om-kanslokarusellen-vi-skrek-vi-hoppade-vi-flog/)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Was on skis from a very early age and also played football as a child, but cross-country skiing proved to be his strongest sport. He began competing in skiing around age 7-8, initially excelling in classic technique. As a child, his idol was triple Olympic champion Markus Hellner: "When I was a little kid my idol was Markus Hellner. I was a real fan." He remembers vividly when Sweden won the relay at the 2014 Sochi Olympics with Hellner on the anchor leg. He attended a ski academy (gymnasium) for high school, then made the unusual decision to move back home to Hedemora rather than staying at a training center. He builds his own roller ski track at home for time trials and training sessions. In 2022, at just 19, he became the first junior ever to win the men's senior national championship sprint title. [source](https://fasterskier.com/2022/06/edvin-anger-earns-the-prestigious-sixten-jernberg-award/) [source](https://nyheter24.se/sport/1130654-allt-om-edvin-anger-langd-familj-och-vm-i-langdskidor-2023)
- **Partner / Relationship:** Was in a relationship with Norwegian Emma Axelsson (from the Lillehammer area) for approximately 2.5 years. They met through Tinder and their first date was a running session. They moved to Östersund together in 2023 -- Edvin's first time leaving Hedemora. The couple separated in 2025. Anger said: "There's nothing bad between us. We're still in touch as friends." Current relationship status: single. [source](https://www.sportbibeln.se/skidor/edvin-anger-har-gjort-slut-med-norska-flickvannen-emma-axelsson/) [source](https://www.nyaludvikatidning.se/2023-05-13/norska-emma-ar-edvins-nya-karlek-det-var-ren-tur-for-oss-bada/)
- **Children:** None. [source](https://nyheter24.se/sport/1130654-allt-om-edvin-anger-langd-familj-och-vm-i-langdskidor-2023)
- **Education:** Attended a ski academy (gymnasium) for high school. No university education -- he has been a full-time professional skier since graduating. [source](https://nyheter24.se/sport/1130654-allt-om-edvin-anger-langd-familj-och-vm-i-langdskidor-2023)
- **Off-Snow Interests:** Runs a YouTube channel called "ANGR EDUCATION" where he breaks down endurance training sessions (threshold work, strength training) for aspiring skiers -- the channel has garnered thousands of views. He is a passionate runner, using it as a key part of his training: he ran the 10km Blodomloppet in 31:37 despite weighing around 100 kg at 1.90m tall. He has a sponsorship deal with mining company Boliden (signed at a ceremony in the Garpenberg mine), partly due to his strong Hedemora ties. He is also sponsored by Madshus skis. His grandfather is a farmer, and Edvin grew up next to the family farm. He has said he dreamed of becoming a farmer and driving tractors as a child. [source](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/cross-country-skiing-swedens-edvin-anger-surrounds-himself-with-a-close-knit-guard-0604/) [source](https://swedenherald.com/article/ski-star-edvin-anger-shines-in-running-with-impressive-performance) [source](https://www.sportbibeln.se/skidor/edvin-angers-nya-beslut-med-norska-flickvannen-emma-oppnar-nu-upp-om-stora-forandringen-jag-hoppas-att/)
- **Notable Quotes:**
  - *"There are so many big names in Swedish cross-country skiing who have received the Sixten Jernberg Award over the years."* — on being honored with Sweden's most prestigious emerging skier award in 2022 [source](https://fasterskier.com/2022/06/edvin-anger-earns-the-prestigious-sixten-jernberg-award/)
  - *"Hanging out with other athletes all the time every day of the year isn't right for me. That's also one of the reasons I moved back home after finishing high school. Here in Hedemora, I have my normal friends and my family right down the road."* — on his grounded, home-focused lifestyle [source](https://fasterskier.com/2022/06/edvin-anger-earns-the-prestigious-sixten-jernberg-award/)
  - *"I have a big engine and a lot of strength, which means that I can ski efficiently at a high speed for a long time."* — on his physical attributes [source](https://fasterskier.com/2022/06/edvin-anger-earns-the-prestigious-sixten-jernberg-award/)
  - *"I think running has disappeared a bit too much in recent years. But I have tried to break that trend. I have always run a lot and always responded very well to that training."* — on his emphasis on running as training [source](https://swedenherald.com/article/ski-star-edvin-anger-shines-in-running-with-impressive-performance)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** Grounded, somewhat reserved, and deeply connected to his roots in rural Dalarna. Despite his rapid rise to world-class status, Anger has chosen to stay close to home in Hedemora rather than move to a major training hub. He is described as empathetic and humble -- qualities his mother attributes partly to the family tragedy of losing his sister Stina. His biggest life inspiration is his grandfather, a farmer, who taught him that "there are no shortcuts -- just hard work, done right from the beginning." He has evolved from a shy junior into a confident communicator, as seen through his YouTube channel. He prefers a balance of intense focus during training and genuine disconnection from skiing in his off time: "I also believe in being either at work or off." His fan club, chaired by his aunt Sofia Anger, brought hundreds of supporters to Falun to cheer for him. Competitors and commentators note his exceptional combination of raw power (1.90m, ~100 kg) and explosive sprint ability. He won the Sixten Jernberg Award in 2022 -- Sweden's highest honor for emerging cross-country skiers -- and won the U23 World Cup overall title in both 2023/24 and 2024/25. He finished 2nd overall in the 2024/25 World Cup behind only Klæbo. ## Technique & Format Profile
- **Classic strength:** Strong — team sprint bronze at Worlds was in classic
- **Freestyle strength:** Elite — anchoring the freestyle closing leg
- **Sprint ability:** Very strong sprint finish — ideal for relay anchor leg
- **Distance preference:** Sprint to middle distance
- **Climbing ability:** Good

## Key Storylines
- 15 WC podiums at just 24 years old — an extraordinary trajectory
- Anchoring the Swedish relay in the crucial freestyle closing leg
- Two World Championship bronzes in 2025 show he thrives in championship settings
- Sweden's answer to Klæbo — the explosive young finisher tasked with the final leg
- If Sweden is close to Norway in the final leg, an Anger vs Klæbo duel would be electric
