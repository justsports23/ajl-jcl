## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Three-time Olympian (PyeongChang 2018, Beijing 2022, Milano Cortina 2026). Best finish: 9th in Men's 4x10km Relay at PyeongChang 2018. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michal_Nov%C3%A1k_(skier))
- **World Championship results:** Five participations. Best result: 4th in Men's Sprint Classic at Planica 2023. 7th in Team Sprint Classic at Trondheim 2025 (with Tuž). [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sector=CC&competitorid=180263)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 1 x 2nd — silver in 20km mass start at Ruka (November 26, 2023). [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michal_Nov%C3%A1k_(skier))
- **Tour de Ski:** 27th overall in 2025/26 (best stage: 6th in the new 5km heats format). [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/results.html?sectorcode=CC&raceid=49549)
- **Junior World Championships:** 1 x 7th. [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sector=CC&competitorid=180263)

## Key Storylines
- Overcame appendicitis surgery in September 2025 — remarkable recovery to be Olympic-fit.
- Most experienced Czech male cross-country skier (3rd Olympics) — the veteran anchor of the Czech team.
- 11th in the Olympic 10km freestyle at Milano Cortina was his best Olympic individual result — peaking at the right time.
- World Cup podium at Ruka 2023 (2nd in 20km mass start) remains the highlight of Czech men's cross-country skiing.
- 4th in WCH sprint classic at Planica 2023 shows he can compete at the very top in sprint formats.
- Known for his ability to time peak form for major championships — coach Jan Franc's assessment.
- Salomon athlete — one of the few top skiers not on Fischer, Rossignol, or Atomic.
