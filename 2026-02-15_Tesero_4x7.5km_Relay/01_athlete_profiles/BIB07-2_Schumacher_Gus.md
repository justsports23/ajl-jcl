# Gus Schumacher (USA)
**Bib:** 7-2 | **FIS Code:** 3530898 | **DOB:** 2000-07-25 | **Age:** 25
**Club/Team:** Alaska Pacific University Nordic Ski Center / Stifel U.S. Cross Country Ski Team | **National Team Tier:** A-team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Born in Madison, Wisconsin; raised in Anchorage, Alaska. Currently resides in Anchorage. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gus_Schumacher_(skier)) [source](https://www.gusschumacher.com/about)
- **Family Background:** Son of Greg and Amy Schumacher. Parents were recreational skiers who exposed him to ski culture from birth. Father Greg was a member of the United States Ski Team. Mother Amy employed a low-pressure parenting philosophy: "I don't think pressure works in most situations. You just want to support them." She watches races across time zones, waking at 3 a.m. to message him afterward, and creates encouragement books with photos for her children. Has siblings (both adult). Speaks English and German. [source](https://alaskapublic.org/news/anchorage/2026-01-29/to-be-the-best-olympian-gus-schumacher-had-to-learn-to-lose) [source](https://www.gusschumacher.com/about) [source](https://showbizcast.com/cross-country-skiing-gus-schumacher-wikipedia-parents-partner)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Learned to ski as soon as he could walk, using strap-on skis in his Anchorage backyard. As an elementary school student, he and a friend would pretend they were competing at the Olympics after ski practice. Trained with the Anchorage Junior Nordic League and then the Alaska Winter Stars program under coach Jan Buron, who coached him from age 8. Swept both individual races at the Alaska state high school championships to claim the Skimeister title in 2017 and 2018 at Service High School. The family spent summers in Kenai for cabin time, fishing, and soccer leagues. [source](https://alaskapublic.org/news/anchorage/2026-01-29/to-be-the-best-olympian-gus-schumacher-had-to-learn-to-lose) [source](https://www.gusschumacher.com/about) [source](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/athletes/gus-schumacher)
- **Partner / Relationship:** No publicly reported partner or relationship. Listed as single. [source](https://enjoywinter.com/gus-schumacher/)
- **Children:** None.
- **Education:** Graduated from Service High School, Anchorage, in 2018. Graduated from the University of Alaska Anchorage in May 2024 with a bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering. Now works part-time for the engineering consultation firm HDR. [source](https://www.gusschumacher.com/about) [source](https://alaskapublic.org/news/anchorage/2026-01-29/to-be-the-best-olympian-gus-schumacher-had-to-learn-to-lose)
- **Off-Snow Interests:** Commercially fishes for salmon in the summer with his family. Rides motocross, goes backcountry skiing, paddleboarding, camping. Also watches TV and plays video games. Volunteers for Special Olympics, inspired by "an amazing special ed teacher" at his high school. Environmental advocate with Protect Our Winters (POW); testified before the U.S. Senate Budget Committee in 2024 about climate change's impact on the outdoor recreation industry. [source](https://www.gusschumacher.com/about) [source](https://protectourwinters.org/alliance/gus-schumacher/) [source](https://fasterskier.com/2024/03/gus-schumacher-went-to-d-c-to-testify-on-climate-change-then-a-senator-dredged-up-four-year-old-tweets/)
- **Notable Quotes:**
  - *"The connection to the outdoors is something that I wish everyone could experience. I wish I could share that... like there's something inside of you that feels a certain way. Having this body that I get to use to move through mountains... it's unbelievable."* [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/cross-country-skier-gus-schumacher-meditative-connection-great-outdoors-fuels-competitive-drive)
  - *"I feel like I'm learning myself more every year. Everything gets easier, but I keep getting faster... better at getting into flow state."* [source](https://fasterskier.com/2025/10/finding-flow-gus-schumacher-on-training-smarter-staying-healthy-and-loving-the-process/)
  - *"When I was younger, it wasn't just like I wanted to go to the Olympics. I wanted to win the Olympics."* [source](https://alaskapublic.org/news/anchorage/2026-01-29/to-be-the-best-olympian-gus-schumacher-had-to-learn-to-lose)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** Deeply introspective, nature-loving Alaskan whose identity is closely tied to the outdoors. Trains without headphones, listening to the world around him -- a practice he initially viewed as "forced meditation" but has come to love. Described as curious, confident, and grounded. Not a "toe-warmer kind of guy" -- often trains in just a long-sleeve shirt even in cold conditions. A philosophical thinker about sport and life. First American to win a Junior World Championship gold (2020). First American man to win a World Cup distance race since Bill Koch in 1983 (Minneapolis 2024). [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/cross-country-skier-gus-schumacher-meditative-connection-great-outdoors-fuels-competitive-drive) [source](https://fasterskier.com/2025/10/finding-flow-gus-schumacher-on-training-smarter-staying-healthy-and-loving-the-process/) [source](https://alaskapublic.org/news/anchorage/2026-01-29/to-be-the-best-olympian-gus-schumacher-had-to-learn-to-lose)
