# Zak Ketterson (USA)
**Bib:** 7-4 | **FIS Code:** 3530828 | **DOB:** 1997-04-02 | **Age:** 28
**Club/Team:** Team Birkie / Stifel U.S. Cross Country Ski Team (B-Team) | **National Team Tier:** B-Team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Bloomington, Minnesota, USA. Lives in Minneapolis with his wife. [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson) [source](https://www.zakketterson.com/about)
- **Family Background:** Son of James (Jim) and Brigitte (Bridgitte) Ketterson. Father Jim ran cross country and track at Carleton College. Mother Brigitte competed in Nordic skiing at Carleton College, earning All-American honors and contributing to the 1984 national championship relay team; she was inducted into Carleton's Hall of Fame in 2015. Older brother Jan competed in track, cross country, and Nordic skiing at Bloomington Jefferson High School before continuing at Dartmouth College. [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson) [source](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/news/new-faces-us-cross-country-ski-team-qa-zak-ketterson)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Despite his family's deep roots in Nordic skiing and endurance sports, Ketterson initially preferred basketball, football, and baseball. He discovered Nordic skiing in seventh grade, inspired by his older brother Jan's involvement with the Jefferson High School ski team. Won the 2014 Minnesota state championship and qualified for the 2015 World Junior Championships. [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson) [source](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/news/new-faces-us-cross-country-ski-team-qa-zak-ketterson)
- **Partner / Relationship:** Married to Julie Ensrud, a Norwegian cross-country skier and skiing coach. Julie grew up outside Oslo and studied sports science at Lillehammer University College, then chemistry at the University of New Mexico and Northern Michigan University (B.S. in Chemistry with minors in psychology and mathematics). They started dating during Ketterson's junior year of college, which he says coincided with his breakthrough in skiing. Julie assists with his training, including lactate testing during treadmill sessions. Her family lives outside Oslo, close to training venues, and Ketterson takes 3-4 day stays at their cabin in Norway. [source](https://fasterskier.com/2026/02/the-long-way-in-zak-ketterson-and-the-patience-it-took-to-reach-the-olympics/) [source](https://fasterskier.com/2024/11/zak-ketterson-his-new-training-philosophy-and-the-road-to-ruka/)
- **Children:** No public information.
- **Education:** Graduated from Northern Michigan University with a degree in economics. Three-time NCAA All-American at NMU. [source](https://nmuwildcats.com/sports/nordic-skiing/roster/zak-ketterson/3858) [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson)
- **Off-Snow Interests:** Trains at Theodore Wirth Park in Minneapolis. Advocates for making Nordic skiing more accessible through his connection to the Loppet Foundation. Unique among U.S. national team skiers in choosing to live in a major Midwestern city rather than the mountains. Enjoys training on Minneapolis's urban trails and bike paths. [source](https://www.zakketterson.com/zak-blog/7-reasons-i-love-minneapolis) [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson)
- **Notable Quotes:**
  - *"It's where I'm from, it's where my family is; my wife and I have a house there."* -- on choosing to live and train in Minneapolis [source](https://fasterskier.com/2024/11/zak-ketterson-his-new-training-philosophy-and-the-road-to-ruka/)
  - *"I am not the type of guy that likes to just live in the woods and do nothing but eat, sleep, and train. I love that Minneapolis allows me to pursue skiing at the highest level while still having some semblance of a normal life!"* [source](https://www.zakketterson.com/zak-blog/7-reasons-i-love-minneapolis)
  - *"I think rather than looking at winter as this really sad time in Minneapolis people should try having a winter specific activity like cross-country skiing."* [source](https://www.zakketterson.com/zak-blog/7-reasons-i-love-minneapolis)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** A grounded, community-oriented skier whose path to the Olympics has been indirect and patient. At 28, he is the oldest member of the U.S. relay squad. Known for his quiet persistence and Minnesota roots. Values normalcy and balance alongside elite sport. Advocates for accessibility and inclusivity in Nordic skiing. His marriage to Norwegian skier Julie Ensrud gives him an unusually strong connection to Norway's skiing culture. [source](https://fasterskier.com/2026/02/the-long-way-in-zak-ketterson-and-the-patience-it-took-to-reach-the-olympics/) [source](https://www.teambirkie.com/zak-ketterson)
