## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Three-time Olympian (PyeongChang 2018, Beijing 2022, Milano Cortina 2026). Best finish: **4th in Men's Sprint Classic at Milano Cortina 2026** — agonizingly close to a medal. [source](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/norwegian-superstar-powers-to-sprint-gold-at-milano-cortina-2026/)
- **World Championship results:** Four participations. Won Men's Team Sprint Classic silver in Trondheim 2025 and Men's Sprint Free bronze in Trondheim 2025 — his first individual championship medal. [source](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/finnish-cross-country-skiing-teams-for-season-2025-2026/)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 3 podiums (1 x 2nd, 2 x 3rd). First individual podium in Falun in classic sprint (March 15, 2024). [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lauri_Vuorinen)
- **Junior World Championships:** 2 x 4th. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lauri_Vuorinen)

## Key Storylines
- 4th place in the Olympic sprint at Milano Cortina is the heartbreak story of the Games — fought for a medal in the semifinal (beat Ogden in the SF) but couldn't replicate it in the final.
- Won his first individual World Championship medal (bronze) in Trondheim 2025 — career breakthrough season.
- Sprint specialist assigned to the opening classic leg — tactical choice to use his classic sprint speed to set the pace.
- Lives with and coaches partner Johanna Matintalo — unique dual role as athlete and coach.
- Semi-professional photographer — a creative soul in a physically demanding sport.
- Third Olympics at age 31 — veteran presence despite being primarily known as a sprinter.
