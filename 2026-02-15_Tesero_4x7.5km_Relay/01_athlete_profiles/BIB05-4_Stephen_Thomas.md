# Thomas (Tom) Stephen (CAN)
**Bib:** 5-4 | **FIS Code:** [DATA NOT FOUND] | **DOB:** 2002-03-30 | **Age:** 23
**Club/Team:** Alberta World Cup Academy / Foothills Nordic Ski Club (Calgary) | **National Team Tier:** National team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Calgary, Alberta, Canada. Currently trains in Canmore, AB. [source](https://olympic.ca/team-canada/tom-stephen/) [source](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/tom-stephen/)
- **Family Background:** Followed his older sister into cross-country skiing. No further detail publicly available about parents. His role model is Olympian Jesse Cockney, who later became one of his coaches. His dream of competing for Canada was sparked by watching Alex Harvey succeed internationally. [source](https://olympic.ca/team-canada/tom-stephen/) [source](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/tom-stephen/)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Started skiing at a very young age, following his sister into the sport. Began competing at age 10 with Foothills Nordic Ski Club in Calgary. *"I was super interested and I just started tagging along with the group and doing my own thing... Then I joined the program and I've honestly just been skiing my entire life."* Progressed to Team ULLR and then to the Alberta World Cup Academy after high school. Had a constant competitive rivalry with Xavier McKeever growing up in Alberta Cups. [source](https://www.rmoutlook.com/2026-olympics/tom-stephen-bow-valley-olympian-at-milano-cortina-2026-11824790) [source](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/tom-stephen/)
- **Partner / Relationship:** [DATA NOT FOUND]
- **Children:** No (age 23).
- **Education:** Full-time engineering student at the University of Calgary. Works as a software engineer during summers at Zone4 (a race timing company). Also runs a small business called Nordic Up, which makes ski stands for better ski storage. [source](https://olympic.ca/team-canada/tom-stephen/) [source](https://www.rmoutlook.com/2026-olympics/tom-stephen-bow-valley-olympian-at-milano-cortina-2026-11824790)
- **Off-Snow Interests:** Fly fishing. Runs a small business (Nordic Up) making ski storage stands. [source](https://olympic.ca/team-canada/tom-stephen/) [source](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/tom-stephen/)
- **Notable Quotes:**
  - *"Stressed spelled backwards is desserts."* — his favourite quote [source](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/tom-stephen/)
  - *"We would be constantly duelling it out in Canmore and around Alberta doing Alberta Cups because we were just so competitive and pushing each other to the next level."* — on his rivalry with Xavier McKeever [source](https://www.rmoutlook.com/2026-olympics/tom-stephen-bow-valley-olympian-at-milano-cortina-2026-11824790)
  - *"It's been a long journey to get here. We had to go through a lot of tough selection races for this team, and I was really close to not making this Olympic team."* — on his Olympic selection [source](https://www.rmoutlook.com/2026-olympics/tom-stephen-bow-valley-olympian-at-milano-cortina-2026-11824790)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** A resourceful and determined young athlete who balances elite skiing with engineering studies and entrepreneurship. Known for his resilience — won the Canadian Olympic trials race just 10 days after returning to snow following a back injury. The first Canadian male to win gold at the Nordic Junior World Ski Championships. Became part of Canada's history-making junior relay silver medal team alongside McKeever, Drolet, and Leveille in 2020. His lighthearted side shows through his favourite quote about stress and desserts. [source](https://www.rmoutlook.com/2026-olympics/tom-stephen-bow-valley-olympian-at-milano-cortina-2026-11824790) [source](https://olympic.ca/team-canada/tom-stephen/) [source](https://www.ctvnews.ca/calgary/article/calgarian-tom-stephen-secures-spot-on-canadas-olympic-cross-country-team/)

## Career Highlights
- **Olympic Results:** 2026 Milano Cortina: 10km Interval Start Free — 9th (first Olympics)
- **World Cup:** Best result: 11th in Engadin, Switzerland
- **Junior World Championships:** 1 x silver

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Freestyle strength:** Strong — 9th in Olympic 10km freestyle, anchoring the Canadian freestyle leg
- **Distance preference:** Middle distance

## Key Storylines
- Breakthrough Olympic top-10 finish (9th in the 10km) — his best career result by far
- Anchoring Canada's relay on the freestyle closing leg — riding a wave of confidence
- 9th at the Olympics when his best WC result was 11th shows an athlete peaking at the right time
