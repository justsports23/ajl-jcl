# Venue History — Val di Fiemme / Tesero

## Overview
The Cross Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal (formerly Lago di Tesero Cross Country Stadium) in Tesero, Val di Fiemme, Italy, is one of the most storied Nordic skiing venues in the world. With the 2026 Olympics, Val di Fiemme joins **Oslo and Sapporo** as one of the only venues to have hosted all three major Nordic skiing events: Olympics, World Championships, and World Cup. The venue has hosted over **400 international races** to date. [Source: Fiemme World Cup](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/organising-committee/the-history)

## Tour de Ski at Val di Fiemme
Val di Fiemme is the **only venue to host a stage in all editions** of the Tour de Ski since its inception in the 2006/07 season (18+ editions). It serves as the annual finale, typically featuring 2–3 stages including the iconic **Final Climb up Alpe Cermis**.

### Tour de Ski Final Climb — Alpe Cermis
- Stage length: 9.0 km total; climb itself is 3.6 km
- Average gradient: 11.6%
- Maximum gradient: 28.0%
- Elevation gain: 420–425 m
- Nicknamed "Monster Bakken" by Norwegian skiers

## FIS Cross-Country World Cup at Val di Fiemme
- First World Cup event at the venue: **1990**
- Regular World Cup hosting through Tour de Ski stages and standalone events
- Also hosts Nordic Combined World Cup stages (23+ stages since 1995)

## Marcialonga Connection
The venue is connected to the **Marcialonga** course, one of the world's most iconic ski marathon events (70 km classic technique, 1,030 m total elevation gain), held annually since **1971**.
[Source: Marcialonga](https://www.marcialonga.it)
