# Technique Fit Matrix — Men's 4 x 7.5km Relay

## Race Demands
- **Distance:** 7.5 km per leg (30 km total)
- **Technique:** Legs 1-2 Classic, Legs 3-4 Freestyle
- **Course:** Demanding, ~272m total climb, technical descents, tight corners
- **Conditions:** Near-freezing (~0°C), possibly transformed/slushy snow
- **Format:** Mass start relay — pack skiing, tactics, sprint finishes possible

## Rating System
- **5** = Elite match (world's best at this specific demand)
- **4** = Strong match (top-tier ability)
- **3** = Competent (adequate for the demand)
- **2** = Below average (potential weakness)
- **1** = Poor match (significant liability)

### Key Observations
1. **Norway's perfection:** Klæbo's 5.0 anchor leg score is the only perfect rating in the matrix. Norway has no weak leg.
2. **Sweden's freestyle strength:** Their freestyle legs (Poromaa 4.5, Anger 4.5) nearly match Norway's — the gap is on the classic legs.
3. **France's Desloges effect:** Their leg 3 score (4.8) is the second-highest single-leg score — but their other legs drag the average down.
4. **Finland's imbalance:** Elite classic legs (Niskanen at 4.5) but weaker freestyle legs (Anttola at 3.0) create a front-loaded team.
5. **Italy's Pellegrino anchor:** His 4.0 score is boosted by sprint ability but limited by 7.5km endurance concerns.
6. **USA's Ogden boost:** His classic sprint silver gives the US an unexpectedly strong opening leg (3.8).
