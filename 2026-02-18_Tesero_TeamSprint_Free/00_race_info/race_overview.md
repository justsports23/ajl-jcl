## Event Format

In the Team Sprint Free qualification, each team consists of **two athletes**:
- **Leg 1 (Red):** First athlete starts at designated interval (15-second gaps between teams)
- **Leg 2 (Blue):** Second athlete starts approximately 14 minutes after the first wave

Each athlete completes **1 lap** of the 1.547 km sprint course. Teams are ranked by **combined qualification time** (Leg 1 + Leg 2). The top teams advance to the **Team Sprint Final**, which is a mass-start pursuit format where teams alternate laps.

## Historical Context

This is the first time the Women's Team Sprint Free has been held at the Tesero venue at the Olympic level. The venue previously hosted the 1991 and 2003 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships and is a regular stop on the Tour de Ski (Val di Fiemme stages). [Source: Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/venues/tesero-cross-country-skiing-stadium)

## Key Storylines

- **Germany** won the most recent World Cup team sprint free event in **Goms (Jan 23, 2026)**, with Norway finishing 2nd and 3rd. [Source: FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/results.html?sectorcode=CC&raceid=49498)
- **Sweden** dominated the **Davos qualification (Dec 12, 2025)**, placing 1st and 2nd. [Source: FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/results.html?sectorcode=CC&raceid=49480)
- **Norway, Sweden, and USA** enter as the top-3 seeded teams.
- This is an **Olympic Games** event — the highest stakes in cross-country skiing.
- Milano Cortina 2026 features **equal distances for men and women** for the first time in Olympic history. [Source: Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/sports/cross-country-skiing)
