## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Kremenets, Ternopil Oblast, western Ukraine. Kremenets is a city of approximately 20,000 people in the Carpathian foothills. The Kremenets Regional Humanitarian-Pedagogical Academy lists Shumbarets as a member of the Ukrainian national team in Nordic Combined among its students/graduates. [source](https://kogpa.edu.ua/en/golovna/structure-of-the-academy/faculty-of-physical-education-biology-and-psychology/department-of-theory-and-methods-of-physical-education) [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kremenets)
- **Family Background:** **Vitaliy Shumbarets** (born 14 July 1983, Kremenets, Ternopil Oblast) is a Ukrainian ski jumper who competed from 2003 to at least 2010. He finished 45th in the individual normal hill event at the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, and his best WCH result was 13th in team large hill (2003, 2007). The shared uncommon surname, shared hometown of Kremenets, and the 18-year age gap strongly suggest Vitaliy is Oleksandr's father — though this is **[UNVERIFIED]** as no source explicitly confirms the relationship. If true, Oleksandr would be a second-generation winter sports athlete following in his father's footsteps, transitioning from pure ski jumping to Nordic Combined. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitaliy_Shumbarets)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Likely trained at the Vorokhta ski jumping complex in the Ukrainian Carpathians (Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast), which houses K-75 and K-90 ski jumps — the highest in Ukraine — and a sports school for winter sports. Vorokhta has been the primary training center for Ukrainian ski jumpers and Nordic Combined athletes since the Soviet era. The Kremenets branch of the regional sports school of winter sports also organizes winter training camps. [source](https://chillax.com.ua/en/the-ski-jumps-of-vorokhta/)
