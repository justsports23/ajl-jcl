# Akito Watabe (JPN)
**Bib:** 21 | **FIS Code:** 100002 | **DOB:** May 26, 1988 | **Age:** 37
**Club/Team:** Kitano Construction Corp. Ski Club | **National Team Tier:** Japan A-team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Hakuba, Nagano Prefecture, Japan [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Family Background:** Brother of Yoshito Watabe, also a Nordic Combined athlete. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Grew up in Hakuba, the site of the 1998 Winter Olympics. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** One of the greatest Nordic Combined athletes of all time. At 37, he is the elder statesman of the sport and likely competing in his final Olympics. Known for his incredible longevity and consistency. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)

## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** **6x Olympian** (2006, 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022, 2026). **2x silver, 2x bronze** [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **World Championship results:** 10 WCH — 1x gold, 1x silver, 4x bronze
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** **79 WC podiums — 19 wins, 31x 2nd, 29x 3rd** — the most podiums of ANY athlete in this field [source](https://www.fis-ski.com)
- **Other major results:** The all-time most successful Asian Nordic Combined athlete. Has competed at the highest level for nearly two decades.

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Jumping strength:** Strong — still competitive at 37
- **Cross-country strength:** Good — was once among the fastest but has slowed with age
- **Sprint ability:** Diminished from his prime but still tactically clever
- **Distance preference:** 10km Gundersen
- **Best format:** Individual Gundersen — his vast experience makes him tactically the smartest racer in the field
- **Climbing ability:** Good

## Key Storylines
- **79 World Cup podiums** — the most of ANY athlete in this start list. A true legend of the sport
- **6th Olympics** at age 37 — almost certainly his farewell Olympics
- Nordic Combined's uncertain Olympic future makes this potentially the **last Olympic Nordic Combined race ever** — poignant for a man who has defined the sport
- Has 4 Olympic medals (2 silver, 2 bronze) — could he add a 5th in his twilight?
- His brother Yoshito has also competed internationally — skiing is a family affair
- Even if not a realistic medal contender at this stage, his presence elevates the event
