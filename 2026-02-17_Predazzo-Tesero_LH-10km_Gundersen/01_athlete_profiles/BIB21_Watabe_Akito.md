# Akito Watabe (JPN)
**Bib:** 21 | **FIS Code:** 100002 | **DOB:** May 26, 1988 | **Age:** 37
**Club/Team:** Kitano Construction Corp. Ski Club | **National Team Tier:** Japan A-team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Born and raised in **Hakuba, Nagano Prefecture, Japan** — site of the 1998 Winter Olympics. Parents: father Osamu Watabe, mother Kayoko Watabe. Attended Nagano Prefectural Hakuba High School. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe) [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/nordic-combined/nordic-combined-news-multimedia/news-multimedia/news/archive/athlete-profiles/article=akito-watabe)
- **Family Background:** Brother **Yoshito Watabe** (born 1991) is also a Nordic Combined athlete on the World Cup circuit. In March 2013, the brothers achieved a historic moment when they both stood on the World Cup podium in Oslo. They won WCH team bronze together in 2017. His wife **Yurie Watabe** (née Yamazaki) is a snowboard halfpipe athlete who finished 22nd at the PyeongChang 2018 Olympics. The couple married on May 24, 2014 and have one son, born November 2020. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe) [source](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/nordic-combined-japan-watabe-akito)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Began competing in Nordic ski competitions in **4th grade** (around age 10). Made his Olympic debut at the **2006 Turin Games** while still a high school student — the youngest in this field by far at that time. Attended **Waseda University's Faculty of Sport Sciences** after high school. His first World Cup victory came in **Val di Fiemme in February 2012** — the same venue where these Olympics are held. [source](https://www.goldwin-global.com/eu/feature/akito-watabe/) [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Partner / Relationship:** Married to **Yurie Watabe** (née Yamazaki), a former Olympic snowboard halfpipe athlete, since May 2014. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Children:** One son, born November 2020. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Education:** **Waseda University**, Faculty of Sport Sciences. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **Off-Snow Interests:** Passionate about **backcountry skiing and backcountry telemark skiing**. Plans to enjoy spring snow in Japan after retirement: *"My hobby is backcountry skiing, backcountry telemark skiing. I'm sure I will enjoy the spring snow in Japan."* [source](https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/others/2026/02/17/olympics-nordic-combined-watabe-savours-sixth-and-final-olympics-as-japanese-veteran-prepares-to-bow-out)
- **Notable Quotes:**
  - *"It's amazing. My hope was to get some medal here but it's okay. I was enjoying this really nice atmosphere and my sixth Olympics."* — after finishing 11th in the NH/10km at Milano Cortina 2026 [source](https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/others/2026/02/17/olympics-nordic-combined-watabe-savours-sixth-and-final-olympics-as-japanese-veteran-prepares-to-bow-out)
  - *"Then I quit my sports career. I want to take time with my family and have some holidays."* — on his post-retirement plans [source](https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/others/2026/02/17/olympics-nordic-combined-watabe-savours-sixth-and-final-olympics-as-japanese-veteran-prepares-to-bow-out)
  - *"I've thought many times that I could retire anytime. But I now think that I'd be the happiest if I push myself 'till the end and quit at the point where I can no longer bloom again."* — on his retirement philosophy [source](https://www.goldwin-global.com/eu/feature/akito-watabe/)
  - *"It is going to be an emotional moment (to compete there again)."* — on returning to Val di Fiemme, where he won his first World Cup in 2012 [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2024-25/last-worlds-for-watabe-as-five-time-medalist-dreams-of-perfect-end-to-career)
  - *"I still enjoy every single day."* — on his motivation to continue at age 37 [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2024-25/last-worlds-for-watabe-as-five-time-medalist-dreams-of-perfect-end-to-career)
- **Personality / Public Persona:** A living legend of Nordic Combined — the most decorated Asian athlete in the sport's history. At 37, competing in his **sixth Olympics** (a span of 20 years from 2006 to 2026), he is the most experienced athlete in the field by a wide margin. Known for his philosophical, graceful approach to competition and retirement. Was Japan's **flag bearer** at the Beijing 2022 Opening Ceremony. Will retire at the end of this season after final WC events in Lahti and Oslo. [source](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/nordic-combined-japanese-legend-akito-watabe-to-retire-at-the-end-of-the-season-1023/) [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)

## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** **6x Olympian** (2006, 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022, 2026) — 20-year Olympic span. **4 Olympic medals: 2x silver** (NH/10km Sochi 2014, NH/10km PyeongChang 2018), **2x bronze** (LH/10km & Team LH Beijing 2022). Japan flag bearer at Beijing 2022. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **World Championship results:** 10 WCH — 1x gold (Team Sprint, 2009), 1x silver, 4x bronze. Five-time WCH medalist. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** **79 WC podiums — 19 wins, 31x 2nd, 29x 3rd.** The most podiums of any athlete in this field. First WC win at Val di Fiemme in February 2012. WC overall champion 2017-18 season. [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=100002&type=result)
- **Other major results:** The all-time most successful Asian Nordic Combined athlete. Two decades at the highest level. Waseda University graduate. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe)

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Jumping strength:** Strong — still competitive at 37 with vast experience reading wind and conditions
- **Cross-country strength:** Good — was once among the fastest but has slowed with age
- **Sprint ability:** Diminished from his prime but still tactically the smartest racer in the field
- **Distance preference:** 10km Gundersen
- **Best format:** Individual Gundersen — his vast experience makes him tactically brilliant
- **Climbing ability:** Good

## Key Storylines
- **79 World Cup podiums** — the most of any athlete in this start list. A true legend of the sport
- **6th Olympics** at age 37 — his **farewell Olympics**. 20-year span from Turin 2006 to Milano Cortina 2026
- His first WC victory was in **Val di Fiemme in 2012** — returning to the venue where it all started for his final act
- Nordic Combined's uncertain Olympic future makes this potentially the **last Olympic NC race ever** — deeply poignant for a man who has defined the sport
- Has 4 Olympic medals (2 silver, 2 bronze) — could he add a 5th in his twilight? LH bronze in Beijing 2022 proves he can still medal
- Wife **Yurie** is a former Olympic snowboard athlete — an Olympic couple
- Plans to retire after final WC events in Lahti and Oslo, then enjoy backcountry telemark skiing with his family
- *"I'd be the happiest if I push myself 'till the end and quit at the point where I can no longer bloom again"*
