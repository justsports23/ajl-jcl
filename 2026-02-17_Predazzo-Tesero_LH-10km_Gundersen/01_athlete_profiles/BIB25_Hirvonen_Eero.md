- **Personality / Public Persona:** A quiet, persistent competitor who peaked at the perfect time. His bronze medal triggered an overwhelmed emotional response in the mixed zone — *"'Phew,' he repeated, searching for words"* (Nordic Mag). Finnish media called it a *"sensation"* (Ilta-Sanomat), and YLE consultant Ville Nousiainen praised his race craft: *"What a run! He negotiated the inside of the bend perfectly on every run."* Coached by Petter Kukkonen as part of the Finnish A-team alongside Herola and Karhumaa. > In the NH/10km, Hirvonen started in 10th place, 36 seconds behind the leader. He delivered his best hill section performance of the season. Before the final lap, he stumbled alongside Oftebro on a climb but recovered. On the final ascent, after Oftebro and Lamparter broke away, Hirvonen outsprinted Stefan Rettenegger to secure bronze — Finland's first Olympic NC medal since 2006 Turin. ## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** 3x Olympian (2018, 2022, 2026). **Bronze NH/10km (MiCo 2026)** — his first Olympic medal! Finland's first Olympic NC medal in 20 years (since team bronze at Turin 2006). [source](https://www.helsinkitimes.fi/themes/themes/sports/28506-eero-hirvonen-takes-olympic-bronze-in-nordic-combined.html)
- **World Championship results:** 5 WCH — 2x 4th (agonizingly close to medals)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** **11 WC podiums — 1 win, 3x 2nd, 7x 3rd** [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=100119&type=result)
- **Other major results:** Won the 2x7.5km team sprint with Ilkka Herola at Lahti in front of their home crowd (February 9, 2019). Junior World Championship medals. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eero_Hirvonen)

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Jumping strength:** Good — solid and consistent, delivered his best hill section of the season at the Olympics
- **Cross-country strength:** **Elite** — one of the fastest skiers in the field; his bronze medal was earned through exceptional cross-country speed
- **Sprint ability:** Very strong — outsprinted Rettenegger for bronze on the final climb
- **Distance preference:** 10km Gundersen
- **Best format:** Individual Gundersen — his cross-country speed compensates for any jumping deficit
- **Climbing ability:** Excellent — thrived on the steep Tesero climbs

## Key Storylines
- **Fresh Olympic bronze medalist** — won his first Olympic medal just 6 days ago, Finland's first NC Olympic medal in 20 years
- Has stated *"These are very certainly my last Olympics"* — making every race count
- Riding a **wave of confidence** and momentum heading into the LH event
- His 2x 4th places at World Championships show he's always been close — now he's broken through
- The **large hill** format increases jumping importance — could challenge his medal chances, but he's shown he can handle Olympic pressure
- Part of Finland's strong 3-man team with Herola (BIB 30) and Karhumaa (BIB 10) — the Finnish hunting/fishing buddies
- Won a team sprint with Herola at Lahti 2019 — these two share a deep competitive bond
- Emotional post-race reaction: overwhelmed in the mixed zone, could barely find words
