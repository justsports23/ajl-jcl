# Kristjan Ilves (EST)
**Bib:** 26 | **FIS Code:** 161576 | **DOB:** June 10, 1996 | **Age:** 29
**Club/Team:** Elva Skiclub / Team Ilves | **National Team Tier:** Estonia national team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Born in Tartu, Estonia. Grew up in Elva, Tartu County, Estonia. Currently splits his time between **Tartu, Estonia and Trondheim, Norway**, where he has trained at the highest level for several years. Nicknamed **"King of Peedu."** [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves) [source](https://www.redbull.com/int-en/athlete/kristjan-ilves) [source](https://www.olympedia.org/athletes/2301715)
- **Family Background:** Father is a **biathlon coach** in Elva — the key influence in Kristjan's introduction to Nordic skiing. Brother **Andreas Ilves** (born April 28, 2000, Tartu) is also a Nordic Combined athlete; his best result was 26th at the FIS Junior World Ski Championships. The brothers founded **"Team Ilves"** as their professional training preparation program, a self-organized Estonian operation that competes against the well-funded national programs of Norway, Germany, and Austria. [source](https://team-ilves.ee/index.php/2018/05/30/about-us/) [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Made his first ski jump at **age four** from an 8-metre hill in Elva and *"instantly fell in love with the sport."* His father coached in the area and the choice to pursue Nordic Combined was, as Kristjan explains, *"a pretty obvious choice for me."* Educated at the **Otepää branch of the Estonian Sports Gymnasium**. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves)

A Red Bull athlete who has built his career from a tiny winter sports nation, training in Trondheim to access elite-level facilities. His presence leading the NH/10km jumping at the Olympics was described as "a big hit with the IOC" — his success from a smaller nation supports Nordic Combined's diversity argument in its fight to remain Olympic. ## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** 4x Olympian (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026). Best: **6th NH/10km (MiCo 2026)**; 9th LH/10km (Beijing 2022). Debuted at Sochi 2014 at age 17. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves)
- **World Championship results:** 7 WCH — best result **4th** (LH/10km, 2023 Planica) [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=161576)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** **12 WC podiums — 6x 2nd, 6x 3rd** (no wins yet — the "eternal bridesmaid") [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=161576)
- **Junior achievements:** Bronze medals at Junior WCH in 2013, 2015, and 2016 [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves)
- **Other major results:** First WC podium in Hakuba, Japan (3rd, February 2018). 2023 Estonian Champion. Best WC overall finish: 5th (2021-22 season). [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristjan_Ilves)

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Jumping strength:** **Elite** — led the jumping in the NH event at the Olympics with 99m. Training jumps of 101m, 99m, and 106m in Predazzo show world-class form
- **Cross-country strength:** Good but not in the elite tier — tends to lose positions during the XC leg
- **Sprint ability:** Average
- **Distance preference:** 10km Gundersen
- **Best format:** Individual Gundersen — his jumping gives him leads to defend
- **Climbing ability:** Average — the steep Tesero climbs could be challenging
