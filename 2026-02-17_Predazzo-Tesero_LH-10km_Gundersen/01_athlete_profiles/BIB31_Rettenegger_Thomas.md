# Thomas Rettenegger (AUT)
**Bib:** 31 | **FIS Code:** 182840 | **DOB:** February 5, 2000 | **Age:** 26
**Club/Team:** TSU St. Veit im Pongau-Salzburg | **National Team Tier:** A-team

## Personal Bio
- **Hometown / Place of Origin:** Pongau, Salzburger Land (Salzburg region), Austria [source](https://www.felbermayr.cc/en/news/detail/brand-ambassadors-thomas-and-stefan-rettenegger)
- **Family Background:** Older brother of Stefan Rettenegger (BIB 32). The Rettenegger brothers from Pongau are among the greatest hopes of Austrian Nordic Combined. Together, they brought victory to Austria in the team classification at the 2020 Nordic Junior Ski World Championships. At age fifteen, Thomas won a bronze medal at an international school competition. [source](https://www.felbermayr.cc/en/news/detail/brand-ambassadors-thomas-and-stefan-rettenegger) [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Rettenegger)
- **Childhood & Introduction to Skiing:** Grew up in Pongau in the Salzburg Alps. Developed into one of the best jumpers on the Nordic Combined circuit. [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Rettenegger)

## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** 2026 Milano Cortina — 9th (NH/10km); first Olympic appearance
- **World Championship results:** 1 WCH appearance
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 4 WC podiums — **2 wins** (Trondheim & Ramsau, Dec 2025), 2x 3rd
- **Other major results:** 2020 Nordic Junior WCH team champion (with brother Stefan). Austrian summer mass start champion (Bischofshofen, 2025). At the Seefeld Triple (Jan 2026), won the pocket jump with a superb 106m leap. [source](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/nordic-combined-seefeld-thomas-rettenegger-wins-the-triple-pocket-jump-laurent-muhlethaler-in-the-top-20-0130/)

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Jumping strength:** **Elite** — consistently one of the top jumpers on the circuit. At Ruka WC opener, soared to 138.0m for the longest jump of the day (137.9 pts). At Seefeld, jumped 106m to win the pocket jump. [source](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2025-26/lamparter-wins-nordic-combined-opener-in-ruka)
- **Cross-country strength:** Strong — can defend jumping leads
- **Sprint ability:** Good
- **Distance preference:** 10km Gundersen / mass start
- **Best format:** Mass start and Gundersen — his elite jumping gives him leads to defend
- **Climbing ability:** Good
